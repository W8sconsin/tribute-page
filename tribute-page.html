A Biography of Taïa

Abdellah Taïa is a groundbreaking novelist and filmmaker. Taïa was born to a poor family in Rabat, Morocco in 1973. He suffered sexual, physical, and psychological violence as an effeminate gay youth. Miraculously, Taïa overcame these many challenges to become one of the greats of 21st century francophone literature, a pioneering filmmaker, and a captivating orator. Taïa's work is semi-autobiographical and looks unflinchingly at issues such as sexuality, poverty, gender, sexual orientation, religion, and cultural intersectionality. As one of the first openly gay Moroccan, Muslim, and Arab public figures, Taïa has created an opening for a new generation of artists to explore what it means to traverse continents and identities.
